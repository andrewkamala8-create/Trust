Methodology

For every listed BTC option on Binance, gamma exposure is computed as GEX = γ × OpenInterest × ContractUnit × Spot² × 0.01 — the dollar change in dealer delta-hedging demand per 1% move in BTC. Gamma (γ) and open interest are pulled live from Binance's /eapi/v1/mark and /eapi/v1/openInterest endpoints; nothing here is simulated, backfilled, or interpolated from another venue. Sign convention assumes dealers are net short calls and long puts (the standard public GEX convention — flip the switch above if you model it the other way; true dealer positioning is never public knowledge, on any venue).

Why not PAXG or EURUSDT?

GEX only exists where an options market exists. Binance lists no options chain for PAXG or EURUSDT — and no other public API exposes one either — so there is no open interest or gamma to calculate against. Rather than fabricate a number, this app only covers BTC, the one asset where Binance provides real options data.

Data quality note

Binance's BTC options market is thin relative to Deribit or CME. Treat strikes with very low open interest as directional signal only, not a precise institutional read — check the OI column before weighting any single strike.
